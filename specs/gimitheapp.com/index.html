Family financials made fun

Gimi has fantastic family features that allow children to handle their own money and to get economic superpowers in real life.

25 $

Keep track of the money

Parents choose day, sum and whether it should be weekly or monthly allowance. Get recommendations about what is an appropriate sum for the child’s age and get notified when it’s time to pay up.

Superpower:

The children take responsibility and plan their spending.

 

10% interest rate

50 $

A live piggy bank

Parents encourage saving by putting interest in the piggy bank. When the child saves money, the piggy bank becomes happier.

Superpower:

• The children learn that saving pays off.

 

Tasks with photo evidence

Parents hand out tasks that reward the children. The tasks can be reoccurring on specific days, demand photo evidence or as first come first serve.

Superpower:

• The children earn their own money and better understand its value

Saving goals for big dreams

The children can plan their savings towards goals and see how they get closer with every penny.

Superpower:

• The children can save up to things they previously only dreamt about
